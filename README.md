# My JNTU Kakinada Experience — 7 Attempts, Still No Degree

I joined a JNTU Kakinada affiliated college in 2020 as a B.Tech (CSE) student, believing it would be a turning point in my life.

In the beginning, everything felt exciting — like a mix of technology, innovation, and opportunity. Subjects like programming, mathematics, and applied physics gave me hope that I was building a future.

But that belief slowly started breaking.

---

## The First Failure

In my 1-1 semester, I failed **Applied Physics**.

At first, I didn’t take it seriously. I thought:

> “It’s okay. I’ll clear it next time.”

That was the beginning of a cycle I never expected.

---

## The Loop That Never Ends

I wrote the supplementary exam again.  
Then again.  
Then again.

Each time:
- I prepared better  
- I wrote more  
- I improved my answers  

But the result never changed.

**FAIL.**

---

## The System Behind It

In JNTU Kakinada affiliated colleges, there’s a belief among students:

> “Once you fail certain subjects, passing them becomes extremely difficult.”

Not because students don’t study —  
but because of **evaluation issues and lack of transparency**.

---

## The Cost of Trying

To “fix” results, students are given options:

- Recounting — ₹100  
- Revaluation — ₹1,000  
- Challenge Revaluation — ₹12,000  

So we try everything.

We pay.  
We wait.  
We hope.

But most of the time, **nothing changes**.

---

## The Challenge Revaluation Experience

Some of us even went for **Challenge Revaluation**.

We took subject experts and went to JNTU Kakinada.

Here’s what happened:

- Our expert said the answers deserved passing marks  
- The university evaluator came **without a proper marking scheme**  
- They compared answers with a fixed “key”  

And then said:

> “These exact words are not in our answer key.”

Even if:
- You wrote 3–4 pages of correct theory  
- You explained concepts properly  
- Your logic was valid  

👉 **Marks given: 0**

---

## Even Diagrams Are Rejected

In some cases:
- If a diagram had an extra ray → 0 marks  
- If a word didn’t match exactly → 0 marks  

It didn’t matter how well you understood the subject.

What mattered was matching an **invisible answer script**.

---

## Inconsistency That Raises Questions

In the same challenge process:
- One student got **40 marks and passed**
- Another student with similar answers got **0**

Same system.  
Same process.  
Different results.

---

## The Bigger Picture

From what we see:

- 1,000–1,500 students per batch per college struggle with similar issues  
- Across 50+ colleges in Andhra Pradesh  
- That’s **10,000+ students stuck in supplementary loops**

Many students:
- Spend years trying to pass one subject  
- Lose job opportunities  
- Lose confidence  

---

## My Situation

It’s now 2026.

I joined in 2020.  
I should have graduated by 2024.

But I’m still writing the same subject —  
for the **6th or 7th time**.

Every attempt:
- Sleepless nights  
- Full preparation  
- Hope  

Every result:
- Failure  

---

## The Core Problem

There is:

- No transparency in evaluation  
- No access to answer scripts  
- No clear marking scheme  
- No consistency in results  

Students are left with only one option:

> Keep writing. Keep paying. Keep waiting.

---

## Why This Matters

This is not just my story.

This is the story of **thousands of students** in JNTU-affiliated colleges.

Students who:
- Studied seriously  
- Worked hard  
- Did everything expected  

But are still stuck.

---

## Final Thought

Education should build careers.

Not trap students in endless cycles of failure.
