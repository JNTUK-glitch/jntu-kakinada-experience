The ₹12,000 Experiment

A real student journey through challenge revaluation — documented, experienced, and still unresolved.

Step 1 — The Failure

A student writes 4 pages of derivation, diagrams, and theory — expecting at least passing marks.

Result arrives: FAIL.

“Maybe I made a mistake. Let me try revaluation.”

Step 2 — The Escalation

The student pays:

₹100 → recounting → no change ₹1,000 → revaluation → still fail ₹12,000 → challenge revaluation → final hope

₹12,000 is not a fee. It’s a gamble.

Step 3 — The Visit

Student travels to JNTU Kakinada with a subject expert.

The expert reviews the paper and confirms: “This should pass.”

Independent expert says PASS. System says FAIL.

Step 4 — The Evaluation

The evaluator arrives — without a marking scheme.

Their logic:

“These exact words are not in my key.”

Result: 0 marks

Different wording = zero. Extra explanation = zero.

Step 5 — The Reality Check

Another student in the same session:

Failed originally → went to challenge → got 40 marks

Same system. Different result.

No consistency. No transparency. No accountability.

Final Outcome

₹12,000 spent. Expert confirmed pass. Still failed.

Time lost: Years Attempts: Multiple Confidence: Destroyed

The system didn’t evaluate the answer sheet. It evaluated whether the answer matched a rigid, unseen key.
